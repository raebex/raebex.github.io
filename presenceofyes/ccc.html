Mission:

Rape occurs on college campuses all across America. The Presence of Yes campaign aims to address, inform, educate, and incite change in policies and how sexual assault is dealt with on campuses nationwide.

Columbia College Chicago's Sexual Assault Policy

In accordance with Columbia College Chicago values and its role as an educational institution, the college reviles any form of sexual offense and will, concurrent with the Clery Act and the Campus Awareness Security Act of 1990, uniformly and consistently report all criminal sexual offenses occurring on campus. Furthermore, it identifies procedures that are encouraged to be considered by a person who may have experienced a sexual offense. This section outlines the College’s procedures:



If you are a victim of sexual assault, take the following steps:

  • Get to a place of safety. Your 1st priority must be ensuring that you are free of any additional harm.
  • Seek the necessary medical attention as soon as possible and/or report the incident to the Chicago Police.
  • Filing a report with the Chicago Police ensures the following and is therefore highly recommended.
  • Eligibility for support services and medical reimbursement per the Crime Victim’s Compensation Law if reported within 72 hours and fully cooperates with police law enforcement
  • An opportunity for collection of evidence helpful in prosecution should you choose to do so. (It is recommended that you do not wash or use the toilet prior to this medical/legal exam.)
  • Assures access to free confidential counseling from counselors specifically trained in sexual assault crisis intervention.


If a sexual assault occurs on campus, students should contact a campus security officer immediately, who can assist with contacting the Chicago Police and Student Health & Support Services.

If the student resides in one of the residential buildings, Columbia College Residential Staff will assist the victim with changes in the academic and living arrangements if desired and if available.

Counselors are available in the Office of Counseling Services to assist with victims of sexual assault and to refer them to community professionals knowledgeable in the areas of mental and physical health, and/or legal issues.
